```
Question 15

Congress passes a law requiring all state legislatures to 
implement specific election procedures for federal offices. 
A state challenges this law as unconstitutional. What is the 
most likely outcome?

A. The law is constitutional under the Elections Clause
B. The law violates the Tenth Amendment  
C. The law is constitutional under the Commerce Clause
D. The law violates principles of federalism

[Submit Answer] [Previous] [Next]
```

### Answer Feedback

After submitting your answer:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Correct Answer">
    <Card title="Correct!" icon="check-circle" color="green">
      Great job! You selected the right answer.
      
      **Explanation:**
      The Elections Clause (Article I, Section 4) explicitly grants Congress 
      the power to regulate the "Times, Places and Manner of holding Elections 
      for Senators and Representatives." While states initially set these rules, 
      Congress has superior authority to override state regulations regarding 
      federal elections. This is one of the few areas where Congress can 
      directly regulate state procedures.
      
      **AI Suggestion:**
      Great job! You've mastered this concept. Continue practicing similar 
      questions about congressional powers.
    </Card>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Incorrect Answer">
    <Card title="Incorrect" icon="times-circle" color="red">
      The correct answer was: A
      
      **Explanation:**
      The Elections Clause (Article I, Section 4) explicitly grants Congress 
      the power to regulate the "Times, Places and Manner of holding Elections 
      for Senators and Representatives." While states initially set these rules, 
      Congress has superior authority to override state regulations regarding 
      federal elections.
      
      Your answer (B - Tenth Amendment violation) is incorrect because the 
      Tenth Amendment only reserves powers to states that are NOT delegated 
      to the federal government. The Elections Clause is an explicit delegation.
      
      **AI Suggestion:**
      Consider reviewing the relevant legal principles about the Elections Clause 
      and the distinction between reserved and delegated powers.
    </Card>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Interactive Features

### Bookmarking Questions

<Info>
  Click the bookmark icon in the top right to save questions for later review.
</Info>

**When to Bookmark:**
- Questions you find particularly challenging
- Concepts you want to review again
- Questions with complex explanations
- Topics you plan to study further

**Bookmark Status:**
- Empty bookmark icon = Not bookmarked
- Filled bookmark icon = Bookmarked
- Bookmarks sync to your user profile

### Show Explanation

After answering a question, click "Show Explanation" to reveal:

- Detailed explanation of the correct answer
- Analysis of why other options are incorrect
- AI-generated study suggestions
- Related concepts to review

### Exit and Save

Click "Exit" at any time to:

1. Save your current session progress
2. Record answered questions to practice history
3. Update bookmarked questions
4. Calculate time spent and score
5. Return to dashboard

<Note>
  Your progress is only saved when you click Exit. Use this button instead of 
  navigating away to ensure your session data is recorded.
</Note>

## Session Data Tracking

Each practice session records:

```typescript
interface PracticeSession {
  sessionId: string;              // Unique session identifier
  timestamp: string;              // ISO 8601 timestamp
  section: string;                // Subject name
  questionIds: string[];          // IDs of practiced questions
  responses: {
    questionId: string;
    selectedOption: string;       // A, B, C, or D
    isCorrect: boolean;
  }[];
  score: number;                  // Correct answers
  totalQuestions: number;         // Questions answered
  timeTaken: number;              // Seconds spent
}
```
