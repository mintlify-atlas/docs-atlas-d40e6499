## Case Briefing

Master the IRAC method with structured case brief exercises:

### The IRAC Framework

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Facts">
    **Summarize the key facts**
    
    Identify and condense the essential facts that led to the legal dispute:
    - Parties involved
    - Actions taken
    - Procedural history
    - Relevant circumstances
    
    **AI Evaluates:**
    - Completeness of fact summary
    - Relevance of included details
    - Clarity of presentation
    - Appropriate level of detail
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Issue">
    **State the legal question**
    
    Frame the precise legal issue before the court:
    - Identify the area of law
    - State the question clearly
    - Focus on the dispositive issue
    - Use proper legal terminology
    
    **AI Evaluates:**
    - Precision of issue statement
    - Proper legal framing
    - Comprehensiveness
    - Clarity of expression
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Rule">
    **Identify the applicable legal rule**
    
    State the legal principle or precedent that governs:
    - Cite relevant law
    - Explain the rule's elements
    - Reference key precedents
    - Provide context
    
    **AI Evaluates:**
    - Accuracy of rule statement
    - Citation of relevant authority
    - Completeness of explanation
    - Proper legal framework
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Application">
    **Apply rule to facts**
    
    Explain how the rule applies to the specific facts:
    - Connect rule elements to facts
    - Analyze both sides
    - Consider counterarguments
    - Show reasoning process
    
    **AI Evaluates:**
    - Logical application
    - Depth of analysis
    - Consideration of alternatives
    - Quality of reasoning
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Conclusion">
    **State the court's holding**
    
    Summarize the court's decision and reasoning:
    - State the outcome
    - Explain the rationale
    - Note any implications
    - Keep it concise
    
    **AI Evaluates:**
    - Accuracy of conclusion
    - Clarity of statement
    - Logical flow from analysis
    - Completeness
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
