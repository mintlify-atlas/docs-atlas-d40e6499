## Conversation Features

### Multi-Turn Dialogue

Have natural back-and-forth conversations:

```
You: Explain the mailbox rule

AI: The mailbox rule states that acceptance of an offer is 
    effective upon dispatch when using an authorized means of 
    communication, rather than upon receipt...

You: What's an "authorized means"?

AI: An authorized means is any method of communication that 
    the offeror explicitly or implicitly indicates is 
    acceptable for acceptance...

You: Give me an example

AI: Sure! Suppose Alice sends Bob a written offer by mail 
    stating "Please respond by mail if you accept." Mail is 
    the authorized means...
```

## Example Conversations

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Concept Question">
    **You:** "I'm confused about the difference between void and voidable contracts."
    
    **AI:** "Great question! This is a critical distinction in Contracts:
    
    **Void Contract:**
    - No legal effect from the beginning
    - Neither party can enforce it
    - Examples: illegal contracts, lack of capacity
    - Cannot be ratified later
    
    **Voidable Contract:**
    - Valid until the party with the right chooses to void it
    - The innocent party can choose to enforce or rescind
    - Examples: contracts with minors, fraud, duress, undue influence
    - Can be ratified by the disadvantaged party
    
    Key difference: A void contract never existed legally, while a voidable 
    contract exists unless and until someone with the right to void it does so.
    
    Would you like some practice questions on this distinction?"
  </Tab>
